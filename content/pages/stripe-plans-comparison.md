## Stripe Pricing Tiers — Which Plan Is Right for You?

Stripe's default pricing is **2.9% + $0.30 per transaction**, but high-volume businesses can negotiate lower rates. Here's what each tier looks like and when upgrading makes financial sense.

## Standard vs Plus vs Custom rates

| Plan | Rate | Monthly fee | Best for |
|------|------|-------------|----------|
| **Standard** | 2.9% + $0.30 | $0 | < $20k/mo volume |
| **Stripe Plus** | ~2.7% + $0.10 | $0 (included in rate) | $20k–$200k/mo |
| **Custom** | Negotiated | $0 | > $200k/mo |
| **Interchange+** | Interchange + ~0.5% | $0 | Enterprise B2B |

*Note: Plus and Custom rates are negotiated directly with Stripe — exact rates vary by business type, churn, and volume. Contact Stripe for a quote when you approach $50k/mo.*

## Break-even analysis: Standard vs reduced rate

The value of a rate reduction compounds quickly at scale. Here's how much you save by moving from 2.9% to 2.5%:

| Monthly volume | Savings at 2.5% vs 2.9% | Annual savings |
|----------------|--------------------------|----------------|
| $10,000 | $40/mo | $480/yr |
| $50,000 | $200/mo | $2,400/yr |
| $100,000 | $400/mo | $4,800/yr |
| $500,000 | $2,000/mo | $24,000/yr |

## What changes at higher Stripe tiers

**Rate negotiation** — The biggest lever. For a $500k/mo business, a 0.4% reduction saves $2,000/mo ($24,000/yr). Stripe's account team typically engages at $50k/mo ARR.

**Dedicated support** — Standard plans use self-serve docs and async support. Plus/Enterprise get a dedicated account manager, faster dispute resolution, and custom SLAs.

**Custom payment methods** — ACH Direct Debit, SEPA, BACS, and local payment methods (iDEAL, Bancontact, etc.) often have separate, lower per-transaction rates at higher tiers.

**Stripe Radar advanced** — Machine learning fraud rules and custom review queues. Reduces chargeback rates, which matters more at scale because Stripe can suspend accounts with > 0.8% dispute rates.

## When to negotiate with Stripe

1. **Volume threshold** — Start a conversation around $30–50k/mo in Stripe revenue. Stripe won't proactively offer better rates below that.
2. **Leverage** — Mention that you're evaluating other processors (Braintree, Adyen). Even hinting at migration often accelerates rate discussions.
3. **Business type** — B2B SaaS with predictable, low-chargeback volume has more negotiating power than consumer marketplaces.
4. **Multi-year commitment** — Stripe sometimes offers better rates for 2–3 year commitments. Only accept if you're confident in the relationship.
